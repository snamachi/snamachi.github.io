About Me

I am a 3rd year Ph.D. student in Logic, Computation, & Methodology at Carnegie Mellon University’s philosophy department. My work draws on insights from formal epistemology to solve problems in various fields of theoretical computer science, e.g. game theory, distributed consensus, and cryptography.

Ongoing Projects

Topological Semantics for Common Inductive Knowledge

A study of the topological models underpinning epistemic logic, focusing on the inductive and transfinite structures of common knowledge. Explores connections between modal fixed points and continuity in epistemic spaces.

Eliciting Causal Bayesian Networks with Scoring Rules

A framework for learning causal structures through proper scoring rules, combining elements of Bayesian elicitation and causal discovery. Examines the theoretical soundness and robustness of elicitation mechanisms under uncertainty.

Resource Bounded Randomness for Instantiating Cryptographic Security

Investigates the use of resource-bounded notions of randomness to justify cryptographic assumptions. Bridges complexity theory with cryptographic security definitions, emphasizing constructivity and computational feasibility.
